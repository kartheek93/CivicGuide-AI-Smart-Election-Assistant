# CivicGuide AI Knowledge Base
This knowledge base is intentionally evergreen. It focuses on stable election guidance and avoids volatile data such as live candidate lists, constituency-specific dates, or changing office holders.

## Eligibility Basics
- A citizen generally needs to be at least 18 years old on or before the qualifying date to vote in India.
- The voter must be an Indian citizen and must be registered in the electoral roll for the relevant constituency.
- Eligibility questions that depend on current registration status should be verified on official Election Commission platforms.

## Voter Registration
- Citizens can register, update details, or verify electoral roll entries through official Election Commission and voter services portals.
- Registration status matters even when a person meets the age and citizenship requirements.
- If a user is old enough but not registered, the next step is to complete registration before polling day.

## Polling Day Checklist
- Confirm that the voter's name is present in the electoral roll before going to the booth.
- Carry EPIC or another Election Commission approved photo identification document.
- Reach the assigned polling station during official polling hours.
- Follow booth instructions and maintain voter secrecy.

## EVM, VVPAT, and NOTA
- Electronic Voting Machines are used to record votes.
- VVPAT provides a short visual confirmation of the selected vote.
- NOTA allows a voter to formally choose none of the listed candidates.
- Indelible ink is used to help prevent duplicate voting.

## Accessibility and Assistance
- Election authorities may provide support for senior citizens, persons with disabilities, and other voters who need assistance.
- Booth-level arrangements can vary by location, so users should verify constituency-specific support through official portals or local election officers.
- Clear, simple guidance improves first-time voter participation and confidence.

## Safe Guidance Rules
- Keep election guidance neutral, factual, and beginner-friendly.
- Do not invent live election dates, candidate information, or official results.
- Encourage users to verify time-sensitive information through official government channels.

## Official Sources
- Election Commission of India: https://www.eci.gov.in/
- Voters' Service Portal: https://voters.eci.gov.in/
- For live election schedules, constituency data, and official status updates, these sources should be treated as the final authority.
